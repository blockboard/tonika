What is Tonika?

Tonika allows a priavte social network of unlimited size to form a secure, independent and full-featured WWW, where every participant can publish and browse on equal basis.

Users of Tonika are equal citizens in a private and secure Internet of their own. Every user obtains a permanent anonymous URL, under which they can publish content or services. Low-level physical message exchange occurs only between pairs of related users in an encrypted and authenticated fashion. Novel routing algorithms establish bilaterally anonymous and encrypted communication between any pair of users.

Why Tonika?

Tonika was designed to address various Internet issues raised in the Internet Manifesto as well as the problem of unfair bandwidth allocation, known as Network Neutrality. And last but not least, Tonika is designed to enable a fully decentralized world-wide social network, where participants have full control over their privacy and anonymity.

The routing algorithms that allow Tonika to scale are a product of many years of mathematical research. Some of the algorithms behind Tonika are now published. See e.g. Electric Routing and Concurrent Flow Cutting.
